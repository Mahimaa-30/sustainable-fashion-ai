🌍 What is EDA?
EDA = Exploratory Data Analysis
EDA helps answer questions like:
Which material creates highest pollution?
Which country has most sustainable brands?
Does certification improve eco-score?
Does higher price mean better sustainability?
Is carbon footprint increasing yearly?
Are eco-friendly brands growing?

This is why companies use EDA before ML training.

MATERIALS TAB 
 WHY THIS IS IMPORTANT

The ML model learns:
Material Type → Sustainability Rating
Example:
If:
Polyester produces high carbon
high waste
low eco-score
then model learns:
Polyester → Lower sustainability
If:
Organic Cotton has low pollution
model learns:
Organic Cotton → Better sustainability

	Charts in Materials Tab
	A) Carbon Chart : average carbon footprint by material. Meaning: Polyester harms environment more. So model gives lower rating.

	B) Water Chart : material_water Shows: water consumption by material.  Important because: high water usage = less sustainable.

	C) Waste Chart : material_waste Shows: production waste generated. Higher waste: lowers eco-score -> affects predictions

	D) Material Count Doughnut : material_count  Shows: how many brands use each material.This helps detect: common industry trends, dominant sustainable materials


COUNTRIES TAB
 Analyzes countries.

 WHY COUNTRY IS IMPORTANT
  Different countries:
follow different environmental laws
have different manufacturing quality
different sustainability standards

Example:
European countries → stricter eco laws
some countries → higher industrial pollution

Model learns these patterns.
A) Country Carbon Chart : Shows: average pollution by country Meaning : Swedish brands may receive better ratings.
B) Brand Count:  Shows: how many brands belong to each country.Useful for: market analysis, dataset balance
C) Country Rating Score
country_avg_rating

This is VERY important.

Encoding Logic

Probably:

Rating	Encoded
A	0
B	1
C	2
D	3

Lower = better.

So:

lower = better sustainability

Example:

Country	Avg Rating Index
Sweden	0.5
USA	1.8

Meaning:
Sweden brands are more sustainable overall.
